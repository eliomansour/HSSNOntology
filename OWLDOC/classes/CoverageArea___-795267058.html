Class: CoverageArea

Annotations (4)

  • rdfs:comment "A Coverage Area is a geographical zone that includes a set of Locations within the range of at least one sensor."
  • rdfs:label "CoverageArea"
  • skos:definition "A CoverageArea is a geographical zone that includes a set of Locations within the range of one sensor. The CoverageArea is defined by a point SL, an angle SA, and a range SR where:
    SL is the SensingLocation, i.e., of the sensor covering the area (depending on the sensor type Mobile | Scalar the CoverageArea moves, rotates, or remains still).
    SA is the SensingAngle, specified in the sensor's technical sheet.
    SR is the SensingRange, i.e., the distance in meters in any given direction such that the sensor is still capable of sensing. This value is also specified by the sensor's technical sheet."
  • skos:example "Example 1:
    For a mobile temperature sensor sens1:
    SL is the location of the sensor
    SA = 360°
    SR = 15m
    Therefore the coverage area is the circle of center P and radius (range) 15m.

    Example 2:
    For a static video surveillance camera sens2:
    SL is the location of the sensor
    SA = 45°
    SR = 30m
    Therefore the coverage area is the sector of center SS, spreading on a 45° angle, and extending to a 30m range."
